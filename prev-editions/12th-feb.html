This week's Fab Resources 📚

I love history. That cliché "To know your future you must know your past" rings very true to me. And when it comes to web dev, people seem to gloss over the challenges that existing solutions were meant to overcome.

The Story of React by ui.dev on the history of React can hopefully provide some insight and perspective on how we got here, and where we might be heading next.

Afterwards, you might have done some of the following: tinkered with React, maybe finished our Career Path, built some projects and you read last week's essay "Learning on the Battlefield" and now wondering 'Michael, how can I train in the most approximated to work environment, in a team with reviews, etc?'.

Until this week, I would advise finding an Open Source project (tricky), getting together with other learners via Discord and Twitter (a lot of admin to make that work) or building a project and dropping a link on the server for people to review (a few reviews, but not as attentive as at the real job).

This week though, I've come across profy.dev. A curious platform, where you get invited to a repo, get a Kanban board, a bot-mentor that does your code reviews, and an example implementation of a project, written by a real human, React dev. And sure, it's still not a 100% work environment, but it's getting closer!

Scrimba is not affiliated with the project, nor was I paid to feature it. 🤣 Give it a go and let me know your thoughts on Discord, or just reply to this email.
